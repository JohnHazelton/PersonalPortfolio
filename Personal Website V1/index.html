  • Studies

    In my undergraduate studies at Vanderbilt, I was able to master soft business skills through collaborative group projects, presentations, and case studies as a Human & Organizational Development (HOD) major. Alternatively, my Economics major led to more quantitative experiences working with statistical models and multivariate analyses both at Vanderbilt and the London School of Economics. Some of my most exciting projects, however, stemmed from my minor in Engineering Management where I solved problems at the intersection of technology and business.

  • Pursuits

    With my varied coursework and experiences, I always strive to find projects at the intersection of free-flowing creativity and data-backed decision making. Using both hard and soft skills in my engineering work in college sparked my ultimate passion for working on the client side of tech. As such, I am pursuing a career that will allow me to utilize my analytical, process oriented thinking in conjunction with creativity. Interning at a Saas startup in marketing has led to my current role at Manhattan Associates as a software consulant, developing skills in data, supply chain, coding, and project management.

  • Hobbies

    Growing up I loved drawing and reading about developments in the tech world. Additionally, I have always been an avid runner, running competitively in high school and more leisurely now as an adult, always searching for races to get involved in both as a runner and a volunteer. The intellectual curiosity from my childhood interests and the discipline from long distance running and training have shaped me into the committed and curious learner I am today.
